01 / The Shoot — What this shoot is

What this shoot is.

Most college sports photoshoots end up producing what get called smile photos. Clean shots against a simple backdrop, athlete posed straight on, arms folded, smiling for the camera. They serve their purpose, but they tend to blend together and rarely stand out.

The shoots I run use dramatic lighting, deliberate composition, and props chosen specifically for the athletes being photographed. Each photo should feel like the actual person in the frame.

The process starts before the shoot day. Athletes complete their profile and the pre-shoot form so they arrive prepared, with their poses and prop ideas already decided. On the day, each athlete works through their choices and their groups in their scheduled slot.

01 / The Shoot — What you get

What you get.

Edited photos, delivered as a gallery each athlete accesses through their email. Galleries are typically ready within 1.5 months, often closer to two or three weeks. You'll get an email when yours is live.

Two kinds of photos come out of every shoot.

Choices — individual photos
Groups — 2–3 to 10–15 athletes per frame
Choices — individual photos

One athlete per frame. These end up on team and athletic accounts, on athletes' personal pages, in marketing materials, and on the video boards during games. Anywhere athletics, the team, or the athlete needs an image of that player, it comes from here.

Groups — small group photos

Anywhere from 2–3 to 10–15 athletes per frame. They have a different job. They're more memorable, and they have more fun in them because athletes are with their friends doing more interesting things. Any coach reading this already knows: athletes get funny in groups. These show up before tournaments, after wins, and anytime a player or team gets featured.

A full team photo comes with any package if you want one.

02 / Packages — What each one means

What each package means for a coach.

The packages scale by how many individual photos each athlete gets. Group photos can be added to any package at additional per-athlete cost. All pricing combinations are in the calculator below.

One thing to know before reading the packages: every athlete needs a warm-up. Photoshoots have warm-up time just like sports do. It usually takes a few photos before an athlete loosens up and looks comfortable on camera. The prep work each athlete does ahead of the shoot helps, but it doesn't fully replace getting in front of the lens. That's why dependable poses work best at lower counts. The more poses an athlete has, the more room there is to push into action shots or complex setups.

02 / Packages — Details

The packages.

1-choice One individual photo per athlete

This package works for teams that want coverage of every athlete without extra deliverables. Dependable poses tend to work best since there's no room for warm-up shots before landing the photo.

2-choice Two individual photos per athlete

With two photos per athlete, there's a little more room. The first can act as a warm-up while the second goes for something more interesting, or the two can offer distinct looks for use on different platforms.

3-choice Three individual photos per athlete

Three photos per athlete is where action shots and more complex poses become reasonable. The first photo or two usually act as the warm-up, and the athlete is comfortable in front of the camera by the third. At this count the scaling actually starts to build in room to try.

5-choice & 8-choice Five or eight individual photos per athlete

Budget is the main difference. These sit between 3-choice and All-Access for teams that want more variety than 3-choice gives without committing to All-Access.

All-Access · Free-choice Practically unlimited photos per athlete

Each athlete gets practically unlimited photos within the shoot time. Within reason, athletes can do as many photos as they want.

Most athletes land in the 10–20 range, counting both individual choices and groups together. That's where they tend to run out of ideas or feel done. That's the working number I plan around.

Group photos are unlimited within the time slot. The team can do as many as the schedule allows.

Group photos 2–3 to 10–15 athletes per frame

Group photos are small group shots. The size varies, anywhere from 2–3 to 10–15 athletes per frame, depending on the team and what they want to do.

Any package can add group photos at additional per-athlete cost. Pricing for each combination is in the calculator.

A full team photo comes with any package if you want one.

03 / Logistics — Booking · Prep · Delivery

Booking, prep, and delivery.

Booking

For outdoor sports, the best window is golden hour — the hour before sunset through the hour after. Any weather in the forecast moves the shoot indoors instantly. I work with $20,000+ in sensitive, non-waterproof equipment, so even a chance of rain triggers the switch. The indoor backup is the field house or event center depending on team size.

For indoor sports, the shoot takes place in the team's own facility. Booking early makes it easier to lock in the right window.

Prep

Before the shoot day, every athlete needs to create a profile in the Athlete Portal and complete the pre-shoot form. This is not optional. Athletes who show up unprepared get worse photos. The form prepares each athlete — poses and props decided ahead of time, personalized to who they are.

On the day of the shoot, each athlete should arrive 15 minutes before their scheduled slot.

Delivery

Most galleries are ready within 1.5 months, often closer to two to three weeks. Each athlete gets a private link sent to the email they entered into their profile. The coach also gets the link and an access code to the full gallery.

04 / Philosophy — Why this pricing works

Why this pricing works.

The pricing is built around the actual work each package takes. Different combinations of choices and groups require different amounts of shooting and editing time, so the per-athlete rate scales with the load.

The math does not scale evenly across packages. A 3-choice package isn't exactly three times the cost of a 1-choice package. I price based on the real work each package requires, not a flat per-photo formula. Some packages are easier to scale up than others, which means the jumps between tiers don't all look proportional on paper.

Group photos scale at a smaller rate than individual photos because each group photo covers multiple athletes in one shot. The editing cost per athlete drops for groups.

For teams with 45 or more athletes, the per-athlete rate drops on a sliding scale, reaching the bottom around 60 athletes. Larger teams get a volume discount because editing efficiency improves at scale.

For preseason shoots, there's a $100 travel fee added to the total.

The rates sit on the friendly end of what this kind of work typically costs.
